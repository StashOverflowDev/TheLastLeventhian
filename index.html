It had been six years, six months, and six days since the clock stopped at 6:06 PM. All counting had become mental. Time had frozen for all of humanity, and with it, seemingly, for Gaia and Zeus as well.

When we first noticed the anomaly, we decided to circle the earth in opposite directions and search for Points of Restoration. Athos — my trusty stallion — decided on his own to go with Hess. I, though I had set out on foot, soon found a young mare somehow also exempt from the frozen time. Several weeks of patient work and we came to understand each other. The ansible — a device for sending messages across any distance, unaffected by what had happened to time — kept us in daily contact. The only technology that still worked across the freeze. We would share our progress each day. Neither of us had found any other sign of unfrozen life.

That evening I found a twenty-floor hotel in Victorian style, at the foot of a high mountain, the other side facing an ocean caught in an eternal sunset. I had even managed to bring the mare up in the service lift. Now she walked a slow circuit of the vast dining room while the echo of her hooves broke against itself every few seconds.

I started the ansible to begin the daily communication. But the flickering hologram revealed nothing more than a low-burning campfire, left entirely unwatched.

I waited nearly an hour, until the battery died completely. The whole time, nothing but that image of an untended fire and no sign that anyone was nearby.

After more than half a dozen years of frozen time, it seemed I would finally spend an entire day without speaking to another soul. And I found that to be mildly frightening.

I. We used to stay high all the time.
That is not a figure of speech for using drugs.
Rather, we had a habit of flying a bit too close to the sun.

Yet, come to think of it, in space there could not be such concepts
as how high or how low we could go.
In space there is nothing like an up or a down.
Those were our mood swings, rocking round,
as we spent time drawing the symbols for infinity. II. Sometimes, instead of shuttles,
I wish there would be trains running through space in fabricated loops,
where train tracks are the famous strings
in a Matrix String Theory of sorts.

At least on those, we would never run out of fuel.
We would probably get bored and end up simply orbiting the sun.

Once there, we would get our mental strength from solar flares,
while nearly all the worries would just stop and stare at us. III. The only worry that would probably remain,
running around our minds much like a hapless child,
is that we could become a tinge too
bothered by the sun.

The lake is still.

It is always still. I have made it still. This is not something I chose; stillness arrived with me the way a shadow arrives, attached, not asked for. The water holds my shape even when I am not in it. When I surface, briefly, I leave a stillness behind that lasts for hours.

I have been here long enough to know the geometry of this place in the way I know my own body. Every ledge. Every depth. The particular cold that lives at the bottom where the light has never reached. I have mapped it with my body because mapping with the mind is something the others used to do, and I am not the others. I am what remains when the others are finished.

This is a fact. I hold it the way the cave holds water. Without complaint. It simply is.

But sometimes, the fact becomes a weight. The weight becomes a sound, and the sound expands.

They are on the shore.

I know them. I have known them since the lake. The younger one, I have been watching the quality of her attention since she arrived at the water's edge. The way she notices things and keeps them. The way she does not discard what she cannot immediately explain. I noted this, and I have been noting her since.

The other one I have known longer. Not personally, but by pattern. Her pattern is old and it has grief in it the way stone has minerals: not on the surface, not decorative, just present throughout, part of the composition. She has been moving toward something for a long time without knowing what it is.

I know what it is.

I am not ready to tell her yet.

The sound is getting louder.

This happens sometimes. When the knowing becomes too much and the aloneness folds in on itself and there is no one left.

There is no one left who sounds like me.

I do not mean voice. I mean the particular frequency of being a thing that changes form because the world requires it, that becomes what the environment needs, that has no fixed self to return to because the self was always the adapting. I mean the loneliness of a creature for whom home is not a place but a species, and the species is gone.

The sound is very loud now.

The lake is moving.

I did not intend this.

I can feel the fabric of the place bending. This has happened before in moments of extremity, but not like this, not this large, not reaching this far. Space is making an exception.

They are on the shore. They are standing in it.

Focus.

I know this word. I learned it from the long listening. Centuries of human voices moving through rock and water eventually teach you the words that matter. Focus is one of them. It means taking the thing that is happening and making it go somewhere instead of everywhere.

I can see them. Both of them. Small and warm against the cold geology. The one who read aloud on the boat. The one with the old grief.

They should not be hurt.

I will not let them be hurt.

The fabric pulls. I pull back against it.

I cannot stop it. It is already happening, it was always going to happen. The pressure has been building since before they arrived, and they are simply the occasion, not the cause. But I can aim it. I can give it a destination instead of just a direction.

I have seen, in the long watching, a place of cold and abandonment and stillness that is not my stillness. A human stillness. The stillness of something that was alive and became quiet. I send them there.

It costs something. I do not have a word for what it costs. The others might have had a word, but the others are gone.

The jungle floor is frozen ground.

The air has changed.

They are somewhere else now. They are safe.

The lake becomes still again.

I make one slow arc through the dark water, feeling the edges of my form, checking that I am still the shape I was. I am. The panic leaves the way it always leaves, not all at once, but in increments, like temperature dropping at the end of a day. Slowly. Then suddenly. Then there is only the cold that was always underneath.

I am the last.

This is a fact. I hold it.

And in the place of cold and human stillness, the two small warm patterns that I aimed and landed safely are already beginning to move.

I watch them for a while.

Then I go back down into the dark, where the water holds my shape and the silence has known me long enough to be comfortable, and I wait for what comes next.

24:45 AM/PM. You might think it would be simple to identify the exact time, but this is not an earthly hour. For us, the day has twenty-five hours. At every midnight, when the world's clocks show 00:00, we stop time for one hour. It is our gift, as gods, to ourselves. We stop time, the planet stops, and all that remains is gravity.

With time stopped, we take the motorbike out — you drive, I never got my licence — and we cover the city end to end. There is no wind, no contact with the air, because time is stopped. A semi-void in which the only thing that still applies is the concept of down. Gravity, that stubborn thing, is the one force we cannot control. And we cover the city end to end, and occasionally we stop and build, or modify. A person here, a building there, so that when our self-imposed hour ends, the city will be slightly different, slightly more to our taste. Our taste. Note appended.

We would go to other places, but even gods such as us have their curses. We can create anything, but we cannot leave our city. I wonder if the gods of other cities suffer the same.

24:59 AM/PM. Our hour is almost over, and for the next twenty-four we will be mortal again. And so it goes. Somewhere in the past, here, at some point, or further along.
